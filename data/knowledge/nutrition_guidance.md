# Nutrition and Lifestyle Support Notes

This content provides general educational guidance and is not personalized medical treatment.

## Vitamin D

- Low vitamin D can be associated with low sunlight exposure, low intake, or malabsorption.
- Typical non-pharmacologic strategies include safe sunlight exposure, dietary sources, and clinician-guided supplementation.

## Vitamin B12 and Folate

- Low B12 or folate can contribute to fatigue and anemia in some people.
- Sources include animal products for B12 and leafy greens/legumes for folate.
- Persistent deficiencies should be evaluated for absorption issues.

## Iron and Ferritin

- Low ferritin can reflect low iron stores.
- Iron-rich foods include legumes, red meat, leafy greens, and fortified foods.
- Vitamin C with meals can improve iron absorption in some diets.

## General Lifestyle

- Sleep: target regular sleep schedule and sufficient duration.
- Activity: include aerobic activity and resistance training as tolerated.
- Nutrition: prioritize whole foods, fiber, hydration, and adequate protein.
- Stress: include practical stress regulation habits like breathing, walking, and social support.
