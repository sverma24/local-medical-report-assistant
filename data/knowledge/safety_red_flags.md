# Safety and Escalation Notes

This file lists high-level educational red flags that may require timely medical review.

- Very high or very low potassium may be urgent depending on symptoms and clinical context.
- Very high glucose with symptoms of dehydration, confusion, or vomiting can require urgent care.
- Markedly low hemoglobin may need urgent evaluation, especially with dizziness, chest pain, shortness of breath, or fainting.
- Significant kidney marker abnormalities (e.g., rising creatinine, low eGFR) should be discussed promptly with a clinician.
- Cardiac markers such as troponin are interpreted in emergency clinical context and should not be self-diagnosed.

Always consider symptoms. If severe symptoms exist, seek emergency care immediately.
