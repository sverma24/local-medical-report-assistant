# Common Blood Test Reference Notes

This file is educational support content for interpreting common lab test patterns.
It is not medical advice and does not replace diagnosis by a qualified clinician.

## Complete Blood Count (CBC)

- Hemoglobin (Hb) low values may be associated with anemia, blood loss, nutritional deficiencies, or chronic disease.
- White blood cells (WBC) high values may be associated with infection, inflammation, stress response, or other causes.
- Platelet abnormalities can require clinical review depending on context and severity.

## Metabolic and Kidney Markers

- Creatinine and eGFR help evaluate kidney function.
- Potassium and sodium imbalance can be clinically significant and should be reviewed by a clinician.
- Glucose and HbA1c can indicate short and medium term glycemic status.

## Lipid Panel

- LDL cholesterol is a risk marker for cardiovascular disease.
- HDL cholesterol is generally protective at higher levels.
- Triglycerides can rise with diet, alcohol intake, insulin resistance, or metabolic syndrome.

## Thyroid

- TSH, free T4, and free T3 are interpreted together for thyroid status.
- Isolated abnormal values should be interpreted with symptoms and repeat testing where appropriate.
